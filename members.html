Members

Meet the team behind URAM-IITD.

The Urban and Regional Air Quality Management group brings together faculty, research scholars, and alumni working across air-quality modeling, environmental systems, health studies, and policy-facing research.

  • Faculty Senior academic leadership across air quality and environmental engineering
  • Scholars Research spanning modeling, health impact, climate policy, and machine learning
  • Alumni Graduates now contributing to research and academic institutions worldwide

Faculty

Experienced guidance across environmental research and practice.

The faculty members associated with URAM-IITD bring deep expertise in air-quality modelling, environmental systems, public policy, and engineering practice, helping shape both research direction and real-world impact.

Prof. Mukesh Khare

Professor Mukesh Khare is a Fellow of the Institution of Engineers India and Fellow of the Wessex Institute of Great Britain. He is a Chartered Engineer and obtained his Ph.D. in the Faculty of Engineering from Newcastle University, UK.

With a specialization in air-quality modelling, his experience spans research and development studies, teaching, consulting, modelling, and editorial activities. He has authored more than 200 research publications and serves on national-level policy and advisory bodies, including the High Level Task Force at the Prime Minister's Office.

Prof. Arvind Kumar Nema

Prof. Arvind K. Nema has 12 years of professional experience in environmental practices for integrated waste management, with expertise in simulation and optimization of environmental systems and environmental impact assessment.

He obtained his doctorate from the Indian Institute of Technology, Mumbai, has more than 50 publications to his credit, and also serves as the Dean of Student Affairs at IIT Delhi.

Prof. Sri Harsha Kota

Sri Harsha Kota is an Professor in the Department of Civil Engineering at IIT Delhi. Earlier, from August 2014 to May 2018, he served as Assistant Professor at the Department of Civil Engineering at IIT Delhi.

He has more than ten years of research experience in air-quality modeling and monitoring. His work focuses on regional and urban air-quality modeling, emission factors, air purification, air-quality management, air pollution and health, indoor air pollution, and source apportionment. He collaborates actively with scientists in India, the US, Europe, and China.

Current Research Scholars

The current scholars contribute to interdisciplinary work across urban air pollution, health outcomes, modeling systems, climate policy, and environmental engineering applications.

Ms. Pratibha Anand

Research Scholar

Pratibha joined the Ph.D. program in the Department of Civil Engineering at IIT Delhi in December 2019. She is a botanist whose research focuses on studying the impact of air pollution, particularly elevated ozone (O₃), on ornamental shrub species using Open Top Chamber (OTC) experiments.

Her work investigates the physiological, biochemical, and morphological responses of plants to ozone stress and aims to identify ozone-tolerant species suitable for urban greening. She has also been part of the project "A Systems Approach to Air Pollution in Delhi," funded by the Global Challenges Research Fund (GCRF), under the supervision of Prof. Mukesh Khare at IIT Delhi. She completed her graduation [B.Sc. (Hons.) Botany] from Miranda House, University of Delhi, and her Master's degree [M.Sc. in Environment Management] from Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University (GGSIPU), Delhi. She is a UGC-NET-JRF qualified candidate.

Supervisors: Dr. Sri Harsha Kota, Prof. Usha Mina (Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi), and Prof. Prashant Kumar (University of Surrey, UK)

Mr. Pareshbhai Dineshbhai Parmar

Research Scholar

Paresh's research lies at the intersection of air quality, atmospheric modeling, and environmental epidemiology. He is particularly interested in understanding how air pollutants such as ground-level ozone (GLO) and fine particulate matter (PM2.5) affect human health, including respiratory, cardiometabolic, and neuropsychiatric outcomes, and is also investigating the socio-economic disparities associated with air-pollution exposure across India.

His work integrates WRF-Chem simulations, data fusion techniques, and advanced epidemiological modeling to quantify the health and socio-economic burden of air pollution. He has also contributed to projects developing a power-plant emission-inventory framework for India and a real-time source-apportionment system for advanced air-pollution management in Delhi.

Supervisors: Dr. Sri Harsha Kota and Dr. Mina Chandra

Mr. Sanchit Bedi

Research Scholar (PMRF)

Sanchit works on developing physics-informed machine learning models to predict the evolution of air pollutants at urban and regional scales. He completed his B.Tech. in Civil Engineering from IIT Delhi before joining M3RG.

He enjoys travelling, sketching, painting, and reading. He is jointly supervised with Prof. N. M. Anoop Krishnan.

Supervisors: Prof. Sri Harsha Kota and Prof. Anoop Krishnan

Mr. Viswanath Velamuri

Research Scholar

Viswanath is an Environmental Engineering and Management researcher with expertise in air-quality modelling — including the Community Earth System Model (CESM) and the Multi-Scale Infrastructure for Chemistry and Aerosols (MUSICA) — emission inventory development, and policy-relevant environmental assessments.

He is experienced in collaborating with national and international research teams to deliver innovative, science-based solutions that support sustainable environmental management and informed policy decisions. He completed his B.Tech in Civil Engineering from NIT Calicut and his M.Tech in Environmental Engineering and Management from IIT Delhi.

Ms. Shipra Lakshmi

Research Scholar

Shipra Lakshmi is a research scholar working in the areas of air pollution modelling, climate change, and the economics of air pollution. She completed her M.Sc. from Jawaharlal Nehru University, New Delhi.

Her research focuses on understanding the interactions between air quality, climate, and socio-economic systems using atmospheric modelling and quantitative analysis. Her interests include urban and regional air quality modelling, emission inventories, climate–air pollution interactions, environmental policy, and the economic assessment of air pollution mitigation strategies.

Supervisor: Prof. Sri Harsha Kota

Ms. Tanya

Research Scholar

My research focuses on investigating the impacts of air pollution on vegetation and understanding the complex interactions between atmospheric pollutants, plant responses, and environmental sustainability. I am particularly interested in exploring how air pollution influences plant physiology, growth, and ecosystem functioning, and how these responses can serve as indicators of environmental change. Through my work, I seek to advance our understanding of plant–atmosphere interactions and their broader implications for agricultural productivity, ecosystem health, and environmental resilience.

By integrating environmental monitoring with plant-based assessments, I aim to generate insights into the effects of atmospheric pollution on vegetation and contribute to the development of sustainable strategies for environmental management and agricultural resilience. My research also extends to evaluating the potential human health risks associated with the accumulation of environmental contaminants in edible crops, providing a comprehensive perspective on the links between air quality, food safety, and public health.

Supervisor: Prof. Sri Harsha Kota and Prof. Arvind Kumar Nema

Mr. Adarsh Choudhary

Research Scholar

Adarsh is a research scholar working in the field of atmospheric science and air quality modeling. He is currently working with advanced atmospheric modeling frameworks, including the Community Earth System Model (CESM) and the Multi-Scale Infrastructure for Chemistry and Aerosols (MUSICA), alongside meteorological normalization techniques.

His research aims to distinguish the effects of emissions and meteorological variability on air pollution, investigating pollutant transport, atmospheric processes, and long-term air quality trends. Adarsh completed his M.Tech in Environmental Engineering from MANIT Bhopal as a Gold Medalist.

Supervisor:Prof. Sri Harsha Kota

Ms. Shweta Gupta

Research Scholar

Shweta is an environmental professional with experience in environmental consultancy, Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA), environmental compliance, emission inventories, and air quality management. She holds an M.Tech. in Environmental Engineering from NIT Tiruchirappalli and is currently pursuing a Ph.D. at IIT Delhi.

She has worked on projects related to industries, agriculture, waste management, and climate change, and is committed to developing practical and sustainable solutions for environmental protection and improved air quality.

Supervisor: Prof. Sri Harsha Kota

Mr. Siddharthan Dileep

Research Scholar

Siddharthan is pursuing his MS(R) at the Yardi School of AI (ScAI), IIT Delhi, working at the intersection of AI and atmospheric science. His research focuses on building operational neural surrogates for air quality forecasting.

His broader interest lies in AI4Science, using machine learning to tackle problems in the physical sciences. He completed his B.Tech in Civil Engineering from NIT Agartala.

Supervisors: Prof. N. M. Anoop Krishnan and Prof. Sri Harsha Kota
